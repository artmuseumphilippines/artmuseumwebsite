NATIONAL ART MUSEUM

The National Museum of the Philippines, also known as the Pambansang Museo ng Pilipinas, is a government umbrella body in the Philippines that maintains a variety of national museums that house collections of ethnographic, anthropological, archaeological, and visual arts objects. From 1973 until 2021, the National Museum served as the Philippine government's regulatory and enforcement authority for the repair and protection of major cultural sites, buildings, and reservations around the nation. Its history may be traced back to the foundation of Museo -Biblioteca de Filipinas, popularly known as the National Library of the Philippines, by the Spanish government on August 12, 1887.

This museum has a collection of art: paintings, sculptures, icons, sketches, mixed media, and photographs. It’s incredible that all of these fine art works were created by filipinos. The traditional functions of art museums to collect, preserve, interpret, and present works of art, and to inspire and educate the public are constantly in flux. Even the definitions of key words such as “art” and “public” are subject to debate, as is “art museum,” which is used variously to describe collecting and non-collecting institutions.
