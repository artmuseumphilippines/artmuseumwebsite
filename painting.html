PAINTINGS

Paintings from the National Fine Arts Collection and other paintings loaned from institutions and individuals designated as National Cultural Treasures and Important Cultural Properties can be found inside the National Museum of Fine Arts (ICPs).

A National Cultural Treasure is any cultural property discovered locally that has exceptional historical, cultural, artistic, or scientific value to the country. An Important Cultural Property (ICP), on the other hand, is a cultural property with exceptional cultural, artistic, and historical significance to the Philippines.

“Portrait of a Lady
(Mi Novia / Portrait of Paz Pardo de Tavera)”
1885

Artist: Juan Luna Y Novicio

This painting was made approximately in the year 1885 but some say it was finished in the year 1890.The woman on the painting is said to be the wife of Juan Luna. The people who owned it over the years experienced bad luck. It was later donated to the National Museum of the Philippines. The artist who made this is Juan Luna Y Novicio.

Recuerdo de Patay
(Memento Mori) of a Child” 1896

Artist: Simon Flores Y De La Rosa

This painting has a size of 61.5 x 86 cm. It was painted in 1902. Recuerdos de patay or in English “Souvenirs of the dead ''. The painting gives us mixed vibes or emotions. If you look at it, it looks like a child sleeping but when you look closely, it is a dead child. During their time, oil portraits of the dead are rare. When photography became popular it became much easier for them in terms of distribution and it is also easily reproduced. The artist who made this is Simon Flores Y Dela Rosa.

“The Burning of Manila” 1942

Artist: Fernando Amorsolo Y Cueto

The painting shows how scary and frightening the battle was. It depicts the battle of the Philippines and United States against Japanese troops in Manila. The artist who made this is Ferndando Amorsolo Y Cueto.

“Rape and Massacre in Ermita” 1947

Artist: Diosdado M. Lorenzo

It has a size of 200cm x 173cm. The painting shows how cruel and harsh the Japanese military were to the Filipinos during the world war. The artist who painted this is Diosdado M. Lorenzso.

“The Progress of Medicine in the Philippines” 1953

Artist: Carlos “Botong” Francisco

It shows 4 paintings of healing practices or on how they cure from the pre-colonial times to the modern period. It has a size of 2.92 meters in height and 2.76 meters in width. This was restored 3 times due to exposure from dust, smoke, insects, etc. PGH and UP loaned the painting to the National Museum of the Philippines so that it can be properly maintained. The artist who painted this is Carlos “Botong” Francisco.

“A Tragic Lesson (The Fall of Bataan)” 1957

Artist: Gene Cabrera

It is a painting showing skulls, reminding us the damage or the casualties that it can cause when we are at war. The artist who painted this is Gene Cabrera.

“Planting of the First Cross” 1965

Artist: Vicente S. Manansala

The painting shows a Spanish people and some natives (pintados) planting the first cross. It has a size of 23 x 24 inches. The artist who painted this is Vicente S. Manansala.

“Noli Me Tangere” 1984

Artist: Leonardo Cruz

This painting was inspired by Jose Rizal’s novel named “Noli Me Tangere”. The painting shows different scenes from the novel. Leonardo Cruz is the painter who painted this masterpiece.

“Doomed Family” 1945

Artists: Dominador Castaneda

The painting shows how families suffered from the harsh treatment during World War 2. You can see a man and a child being tied and a dead woman lying on the floor. This was painted by Dominador Castaneda.
