SKETCHES/DRAWINGS

Watercolor, pastel, and charcoal drawings by notable Filipino artists from various periods in Philippine art history are housed in the National Fine Arts Collection (NFAC). An 1879 self-portrait charcoal drawing by master painter Juan Luna y Novicio from the Spanish colonial period is one of the collection's earliest drawings. A number of graphite sketches by National Artist Fernando Amorsolo, one of the notable artists who excelled in figurative art during the American colonial period, are also included.

Juan Luna y Novicio Self Portrait 1879

Artist: Alejo Vera

This charcoal self-portrait of master painter Juan Luna y Novicio was created in 1879 in Europe by Spanish artist Alejo Vera on commission. This painting was completed five years before Luna received a gold medal for Spoliarium at the 1884 Madrid Art Exposition.
